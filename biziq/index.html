Models & Algorithms Explained

Linear Regression (OLS)
ŷ = β₀ + β₁x
Used to model revenue trend. Ordinary Least Squares minimizes the sum of squared residuals to find the best-fit line through historical revenue data.
Exponential Smoothing
S_t = αy_t + (1−α)S_{t−1}
Gives more weight to recent observations (α=0.3). Used as a secondary model in the forecast ensemble to smooth out noise.
RFM Scoring
Score = R×0.3 + F×0.35 + M×0.35
Each customer is scored on Recency (last purchase), Frequency (order count), and Monetary (total spend). Weighted sum determines segment tier.
Customer LTV
LTV = AOV × F × (1/(1−r))
Lifetime Value calculated from Average Order Value, purchase frequency, and retention rate (r). Assumes geometric series for future purchase probability.
Confidence Interval
CI = ŷ ± 1.96 × σ/√n
95% confidence bands around forecast. Standard error estimated from residuals of the regression fit on historical data.
ROAS & CAC
ROAS = Revenue / AdSpend
CAC = AdSpend / NewCustomers
Return on Ad Spend and Customer Acquisition Cost are core marketing efficiency metrics used to evaluate and optimize paid acquisition channels.
